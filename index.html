The following simulation animations illustrate the dynamic behavior of chromatin condensates as predicted by our model. 25bp chromatin (pink) and similar NRLs following the 10N+5 pattern exhibit more dynamic, liquid like behavior as single fibers. This is explained by the DNA twist coming from the +5 (a half turn) limiting face-face stacking interaction. In contrast, NRLs close to the 10N pattern e.g. 30bp (green), have a much less varied conformational ensemble as dilute fibers, as the full turns in the 10N linker promote the strong face-face stacking interactions..
